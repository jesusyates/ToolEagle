## How to Write TikTok Hooks That Get Views

1. Start with a bold, counterintuitive statement that challenges common belief.
2. Pose a provocative question that your target audience secretly wants answered.
3. Use a 'before and after' hook to showcase a dramatic transformation possibility.
4. Lead with a specific, relatable mistake that you see everyone making.
5. Frame your hook as a secret or a hack that insiders supposedly use.
6. Begin with 'I bet you didn't know...' to instantly spark curiosity.
7. State a surprising statistic or number that feels almost unbelievable.
8. Ask 'What if I told you...' to create an immediate knowledge gap.
9. Use a 'stop doing X, start doing Y' structure for clear, actionable advice.
10. Hook with a mini-story setup: 'The day I realized...' or 'This one time...'
11. Address a universal pain point with 'Tired of...?' or 'Struggling with...?'
12. Create urgency with time-based hooks like 'In the next 60 seconds...'
13. Use comparison hooks: 'The difference between amateurs and pros is...'
14. Promise a quick list or a simple number of steps to achieve a result.
15. Start with a confession or an admission of a past failure for relatability.
16. Employ sensory language: 'The sound you DON'T want to hear from your car...'
17. Hint at a consequence: 'If you skip this step, your project will fail.'
18. Use 'This is why you're failing at...' to directly address viewer frustration.
19. Pose a 'yes or no' question that forces a quick mental self-assessment.
20. Begin with a command: 'Never say this in a job interview again.'
21. Hook with a controversial ranking: 'The most overrated tool for creators is...'
22. Use 'Let me save you 100 hours...' to promise massive time efficiency.
23. Start with a relatable scenario: 'You've just finished editing your video and...'
24. Create intrigue with a partial reveal: 'The one ingredient chefs add to...'
25. Ask for a show of hands: 'Who else has dealt with a client who...?'
26. Use 'I wish someone had told me sooner...' to imply valuable, hard-won insight.
27. Frame advice as a warning: 'Most people ruin their sourdough by doing this.'
28. Start with a paradoxical statement that seems to contradict logic.
29. Hook with a specific audience call-out: 'Attention freelance graphic designers...'
30. Use a 'if you only learn one thing, make it this' structure for importance.
31. Pose a 'why does nobody talk about...?' question to highlight hidden knowledge.
32. Begin with a strong analogy that simplifies a complex topic.
33. Create a 'choose your own adventure' hook with two distinct outcomes.
34. Use 'I tested 10 methods so you don't have to. Here's the winner.'
35. Hook with a simple, powerful truth: 'Great storytelling isn't about complexity.'
36. Start with a common myth and immediately promise to debunk it.
37. Use a 'this changed everything for me' hook to imply transformative potential.
38. Address the viewer directly with 'You're probably using this tool wrong.'
39. Pose a 'can you guess...?' challenge to engage competitive viewers.
40. Hook with an unexpected pairing: 'What military strategy can teach us about content.'
41. Use a 'from zero to...' structure to promise a progression or result.
42. Start with a sensory deprivation hook: 'Close your eyes and imagine...'
43. Create a mini-mystery: 'There's a reason your videos aren't getting shared.'
44. Use a 'forget everything you know about...' hook to signal a paradigm shift.
45. Hook with a specific timestamp of failure or success: 'At 3:17 AM, I knew...'
46. Pose a 'would you rather...?' question tailored to your niche's dilemmas.
47. Start with an over-the-top claim, then immediately ground it with 'here's how.'
48. Use a 'the real reason...' hook to suggest you'll uncover root causes.
49. Address imposter syndrome: 'You know more than you think you do about...'
50. Hook with a simple, direct command followed by a compelling reason: 'Watch this.'

## Publish-ready package framing (what to paste)

### Hook
Most creators write hooks backwards. Here's the simple three-part formula that aligns with how the TikTok brain actually works.

### Script beats
- Open with the core problem or desire your viewer recognizes instantly.
- Introduce the common, flawed approach they're likely already trying.
- Present your alternative method or insight as a clearer, simpler path.
- Quickly demonstrate one tangible proof point or result of using this method.
- Tease the value of watching the full video for the complete framework.
