## How to Write Viral Hooks That Stop the Scroll

1. Start with a bold, specific claim about a common pain point.
2. Ask a 'yes or no' question that your target audience must answer.
3. Use a shocking statistic that contradicts popular belief in your niche.
4. Begin with 'Stop doing X' to immediately challenge a common behavior.
5. Frame the hook as a secret or hack the industry doesn't share.
6. Pose a 'what if' scenario that promises a desirable outcome.
7. Use a quick, relatable story setup: 'I used to struggle with...'
8. Make a comparison between a bad method and your better solution.
9. Start with 'The biggest mistake I see in [niche] is...'
10. Ask 'Why does everyone do X when Y is clearly better?'
11. Use a time-based hook: 'In the next 30 seconds, I'll show...'
12. Begin with a strong visual cue: 'Look at what happens when...'
13. Challenge your viewer directly: 'If you can't do X, watch this.'
14. Use a 'before and after' promise in the opening line.
15. Start with a controversial opinion that sparks curiosity in your field.
16. Ask a 'how to' question that addresses a specific, frustrating task.
17. Use a numbered list hook: '3 things you're getting wrong about...'
18. Begin with a sensory command: 'Listen to the sound of...'
19. Frame it as a public service announcement for your niche community.
20. Start with a relatable confession of a past failure or oversight.
21. Use a 'day in the life' setup that highlights a problem you solve.
22. Pose a 'which one are you?' question with two clear archetypes.
23. Begin with a trending audio snippet that sets up an immediate question.
24. Use a 'if you only learn one thing today, let it be this' frame.
25. Start with a demonstration of a frustrating result from a common method.
26. Ask 'Have you ever noticed that...' about a niche-specific pattern.
27. Use a cost-focused hook: 'Why spending money on X is a waste.'
28. Begin with a quick, satisfying visual result, then promise to teach it.
29. Frame it as a reaction: 'When I see people do X, I cringe.'
30. Start with a myth you're about to debunk in your area of expertise.
31. Use a 'no one tells you about...' statement for insider knowledge.
32. Begin with a simple command: 'Pause if you've ever dealt with X.'
33. Ask a 'why is this so hard?' question about a niche task.
34. Use a 'signs you're doing X wrong' checklist in your opening.
35. Start with a surprising fact about a tool or resource everyone uses.
36. Frame it as a shortcut that saves time on a tedious process.
37. Begin with a side-by-side comparison of outcomes from different choices.
38. Use a 'I wish someone had told me this sooner' sentiment.
39. Start with a question about a symptom that points to a root cause.
40. Use a 'this changed everything for me' statement about a specific tactic.
41. Begin by naming and shaming a vague piece of common advice.
42. Ask 'What's the one thing holding you back from...?'
43. Use a 'let me simplify this confusing topic for you' approach.
44. Start with a prediction: 'This will be the biggest trend in...'
45. Frame it as a fix for a problem viewers think is unsolvable.
46. Begin with a quick, unexpected result that seems like magic.
47. Use a 'the truth about...' statement to counter misinformation.
48. Start with a relatable scenario of overwhelm in your specific niche.
49. Ask 'Do you make this common formatting error in your captions?'
50. Use a 'follow for more' implied hook by giving immense value immediately.
