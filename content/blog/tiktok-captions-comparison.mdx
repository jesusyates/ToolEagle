## Comparison Captions

1. Use a rhetorical question to spark immediate viewer engagement.
2. Start with 'POV:' to drop viewers directly into a relatable scenario.
3. Employ a two-part caption setup with a hook and a payoff.
4. Leverage trending audio by explaining its context in your caption.
5. Create urgency with time-sensitive language like 'before it's gone.'
6. Frame your caption as a secret you're only sharing with followers.
7. Use all caps strategically for one or two impactful words only.
8. Incorporate a numbered list within your caption for easy scanning.
9. Ask for a simple emoji response to boost comment metrics quickly.
10. Pair a surprising fact with a call to verify it in the comments.
11. Start with 'Stop scrolling!' to command attention in the first line.
12. Use brackets to add context or a whispered aside to the viewer.
13. Create a mini-challenge or dare directly within your caption text.
14. Address a specific niche pain point in your very first sentence.
15. End with an open-ended question that prompts story sharing.
16. Use 'Caption this for me' to generate engagement with zero effort.
17. Spoil the video's ending in the caption to create curiosity.
18. Write your caption as if you're texting a friend for authenticity.
19. Include a very specific detail to attract a highly targeted audience.
20. Start with 'Why nobody tells you...' to frame insider knowledge.
21. Use the caption to explain why this trend is relevant to your niche.
22. Pose a 'this or that' style question related to your video content.
23. Hint at a part two or follow-up video to drive profile visits.
24. Add a one-word emoji summary at the start for visual punch.
25. Use the caption to correct a common misconception in your field.
26. Frame the video as a solution to a problem they searched for.
27. Start with 'If you do this, then...' for personalized conditional hooks.
28. Use asterisks to create the effect of real-time action or thought.
29. Directly tag a friend in the caption to encourage shares.
30. End with 'Save this for later' to directly prompt the save action.
