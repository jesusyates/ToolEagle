## First Line Captions

1. Start your caption with a bold statement that challenges common belief.
2. Ask a question that directly addresses your viewer's immediate problem.
3. Use a time-based hook like 'If you do this in the next 24 hours...'
4. Frame your first line as a secret or hack they haven't heard before.
5. Begin with 'Stop doing X' to immediately command attention and curiosity.
6. Lead with a relatable frustration that your target audience experiences daily.
7. Pose a hypothetical scenario that places the viewer in a desirable situation.
8. Start with a surprising statistic or little-known fact about your niche.
9. Use a 'before and after' promise in the very first few words.
10. Open with a controversial opinion that sparks debate within your community.
11. Hook them with a 'This one trick...' style opener for quick-win content.
12. Begin by calling out a specific type of person, e.g., 'For my overthinkers...'
13. Use a 'signs you might be...' format to trigger self-identification.
14. Start with a sensory description, like 'The sound of perfectly crispy fries...'
15. Lead with a nostalgic reference that your core demographic will recognize.
16. Open with a current trend or meme format to show immediate relevance.
17. Use a 'Raise your hand if...' prompt to foster instant engagement.
18. Begin with a false assumption that you are about to debunk.
19. Hook with a 'You've been told X, but the truth is Y' contradiction.
20. Start with a short, powerful story opening: 'Yesterday, I almost quit...'
21. Use a 'If you only learn one thing today, let it be this...' structure.
22. Begin by stating the biggest mistake beginners make in your field.
23. Open with a comparison between two common choices or methods.
24. Hook with a 'I tested X so you don't have to' result-driven statement.
25. Start with a 'Remember when...' to tap into shared experiences or memories.
26. Use a 'No one is talking about...' opener to present novel information.
27. Begin with a simple, direct command: 'Save this for your next trip.'
28. Open with a 'POV:' setup to drop the viewer into a specific scenario.
29. Hook by stating the exact time it takes to get a result, e.g., 'In 60 seconds...'
30. Start with a 'Would you rather...' question to force a quick choice.

### Hook

### Script beats
- Identify the core problem or desire of your viewer.
- Choose a hook formula that matches your content's intent.
- Deliver the promise immediately in the video's first frames.
- Use the rest of the caption to expand and add context.
- End with a clear call to action related to the hook.
