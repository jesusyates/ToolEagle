## Tips Hashtags

1. Use a mix of broad and niche hashtags for balanced reach.
2. Place your most important hashtags in the first comment.
3. Research hashtags your target audience actually searches for.
4. Create a unique branded hashtag for your channel community.
5. Analyze competitor hashtags to identify high-potential keywords.
6. Limit your hashtags to a focused set of five to eight.
7. Incorporate trending hashtags relevant to your video's topic.
8. Use hashtags that describe the problem your content solves.
9. Include hashtags for your specific video format like #shorts.
10. Add location-based hashtags if your content is geographically relevant.
11. Test different hashtag sets in your first five videos.
12. Utilize hashtags that reflect the emotion of your content.
13. Combine evergreen hashtags with timely, trending ones.
14. Avoid overly generic hashtags with billions of views.
15. Check hashtag search volume using YouTube's search suggestions.
16. Use hashtags in your video title for added keyword strength.
17. Create a hashtag series for a multi-part content series.
18. Leverage holiday and event hashtags well in advance.
19. Include hashtags for your primary content category or niche.
20. Add hashtags that specify your audience, like #forbeginners.
21. Use a tool to generate hashtag variations from a seed keyword.
22. Monitor which hashtags drive the most impressions over time.
23. Incorporate relevant community hashtags to join conversations.
24. Avoid banned or spammy hashtags that can hurt visibility.
25. Place two to three core hashtags directly in the video description.
26. Align your YouTube hashtags with your other platform strategies.
27. Use hashtags to highlight the key tools you used in the video.
28. Add hashtags that describe the style of your content creation.
29. Rotate your secondary hashtags to test new audience segments.
30. End your description with a clean block of selected hashtags.
