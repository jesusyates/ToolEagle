## YouTube Script Structure That Keeps People Watching

1. Your first eight seconds must answer the viewer's 'Why should I care?'
2. State the core problem or desire your video will address immediately.
3. Use a bold question or a surprising statistic to halt the scroll.
4. Preview the key benefit viewers will gain by sticking around.
5. Avoid lengthy personal introductions before delivering the core value.
6. Clearly outline the three main points you'll cover in the video.
7. Each beat should transition smoothly using a verbal bridge or visual cue.
8. The first beat after the hook should deliver immediate, tangible value.
9. Structure beats like chapters, each with a mini-conflict and resolution.
10. Use screen text or graphics to reinforce your main talking points.
11. Pace your beats to match your niche's expected viewer attention span.
12. For tutorials, the first beat is often showing the final result.
13. For commentary, your first beat might be presenting the controversial clip.
14. Incorporate a pattern interrupt, like a quick cut or sound effect, between beats.
15. Your middle beat should deepen the explanation or introduce a twist.
16. The final beat should seamlessly set up your call to action.
17. Write for the algorithm by including keywords naturally in each beat.
18. Read your script aloud to catch awkward phrasing and pacing issues.
19. Time each beat to ensure your video's pacing feels dynamic.
20. Use active voice and strong verbs to keep the energy high.
21. Incorporate rhetorical questions within beats to engage the viewer's mind.
22. Place your most compelling evidence or visuals in the second beat.
23. For listicles, each beat is a new item with its own hook.
24. Anticipate and answer potential viewer objections within your script beats.
25. A strong title often mirrors the promise made in your hook.
26. Incorporate your primary keyword in the first three words of the title.
27. Use title case and avoid clickbait that doesn't match the content.
28. Your thumbnail should visually represent the conflict in your title.
29. For captions, use proper capitalization and punctuation for readability.
30. Add captions for sound effects and music cues where relevant.
31. Break long caption blocks into two-line segments for easier viewing.
32. Use emojis sparingly in captions to emphasize points, not decorate.
33. Synchronize caption highlights with the spoken emphasis in your audio.
34. Position your CTA in the final 20 seconds of your video.
35. Make your CTA a single, clear, and actionable next step.
36. Use direct language like 'Click the link below' or 'Comment your answer.'
37. Offer an incentive for completing the CTA, like a free guide.
38. Place an end screen with clickable elements to support your verbal CTA.
39. A soft CTA can be a question that prompts comments and discussion.
40. For series, your CTA should preview the topic of the next video.
41. Hashtags should include a mix of broad niche tags and specific phrases.
42. Place 3-5 highly relevant hashtags in your video's primary description.
43. Research trending hashtags within your niche for potential extra reach.
44. Create a unique, branded hashtag for your channel or series.
45. Avoid overstuffing hashtags; relevance always beats quantity for the algorithm.
46. This structure works because it respects the viewer's limited attention span.
47. It provides clear value early, reducing the likelihood of early drop-offs.
48. The beat-based format creates a satisfying, complete viewing experience.
49. A strong CTA capitalizes on the engagement built throughout the video.
50. Consistent structure trains your audience to expect and enjoy your content format.

### Script beats
- Open with a hook that states a problem or makes a bold promise.
- Deliver your core value in 2-3 clear, structured segments or steps.
- Weave retention elements like visuals and questions throughout each beat.
- End with a strong, single-focused call to action for viewers.
